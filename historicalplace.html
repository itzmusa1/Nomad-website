AXUM

The Axum obelisks are a group of tall, carved stone pillars located in the ancient city of Axum in northern Ethiopia. These obelisks date back to the ancient Kingdom of Axum and are believed to have been erected as early as the 4th century AD. They served as markers of graves or important locations and were symbols of the power and wealth of the Axumite rulers. The largest obelisk, known as the Great Stele, is over 24 meters tall and weighs around 160 tons. The Axum obelisks are UNESCO World Heritage Sites and are significant cultural and historical landmarks in Ethiopia.

FASILIDES PALACE

Fasil Ghebbi, also known as Fasilides Palace, is a royal enclosure located in Gondar, Ethiopia. It was built in the 17th century by Emperor Fasilides and served as the residence of Ethiopian emperors for over 200 years. The palace complex includes several buildings, courtyards, and gardens, showcasing a unique blend of Ethiopian, Indian, and Portuguese architectural styles. Fasil Ghebbi is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a popular tourist attraction in Ethiopia.
Round Trip: 100$

JEGOL PALACE

The Jegol Palace is a historical site located in Harar, Ethiopia. It was built in the late 19th century by the Emir of Harar, Nur ibn Mujahid, as his residence and administrative center. The palace is known for its unique architectural style, featuring intricate carvings, colorful artwork, and traditional Harari design elements. The Jegol Palace has served as a symbol of Harari cultural heritage and history, showcasing the rich heritage of the region. Visitors to the palace can explore its various rooms, courtyards, and gardens, gaining insight into the lifestyle and traditions of the Emir and the people of Harar. Today, the Jegol Palace is a popular tourist attraction in Harar, offering visitors a glimpse into the past and an opportunity to appreciate the beauty and significance of this historic site.
Round Trip: 100$
